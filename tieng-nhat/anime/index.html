    Medium ハンター×ハンター The arcs themselves can vary a lot in difficulty, from easy-medium to medium-hard so medium seems decent as a difficulty marker. There's shonen lingo as well as general fantasy vocabulary.
    Medium デス・パレード The main character uses keigo and the subject matter is more complex than basic slice of life or romcom anime, but it is still not extremely difficult. While the plot is important, the various games are interesting on their own.
    Medium ピンポン THE ANIMATION While the vocabulary isn't too complicated, some characters speak in Chinese with Japanese subtitles and the dialogues can get a bit philosophical at times. Obviously there's some ping pong terminology, although ping pong isn't actually the main theme of this anime.
    Medium 地獄学園 One character speaks like a samurai, there is some wordplay and the characters can talk a bit fast at times. Overall not too hard, but not quite very easy either. Still, the comedy is so good that even if you don't fully understand what's going on you might like it nonetheless.
    Medium ブルーロック Some characters have their own speaking style where add things to the ends of their sentences or use slightly rarer words so it can be a touch harder to pick up on what they say.
    Medium 闘牌伝説アカギ　闇に舞い降りた天才 Knowing Mahjong is an absolute requirement for this one or you will be completely lost. Other than this, medium difficulty.
    Medium-Hard 逆境無頼カイジ Ultimate Survivor Just like above (it's the same author), this centers on mahjong with a strong focus on psychology. It is slightly more difficult though, because you need to pretty accurately understand the rules of each gamble in order to fully enjoy it and there is fairly uncommon vocabulary used by the narrator in Kaiji's particular style of writing.
    Medium-Hard 約束のネバーランド The grammar is very complicated sometimes, and the vocabulary is not that easy. However, the narrative is clear as water, and the dialogues are very well written. It seems like you're reading a novel only made with dialogues. With Violet, even if the vocabulary is sometimes abstract (because of the story), you can almost always keep understanding because of the clearly written dialogue.
    Medium-Hard 新世紀エヴァンゲリオン The anime varies in difficult, in some episodes it is rather easy, towards the end it becomes pretty difficult with intricate dialogue discussing somewhat deep ideas. The mecha and technological terms can be a bit hard at first as well.

    The rebuilds (movies for Evangelion) are roughly of the same difficulty.
    Medium-Hard ヱヴァンゲリヲン新劇場版:序 See above.
    Medium-Hard 魔法科高校の劣等生 Miyuki speaks in very polite speech, lots of technical words, the magic system is very complete but also technological in nature and thus sometimes a bit hard to follow.
    Hard さよなら絶望先生 Constant cultural references, tricky Japanese from time to time with a weird cast makes this one harder than your average anime.
    Very Hard 範馬刃牙 SON OF OGRE The anime is full of phraseology, long dialogues that use words or vocabulary that are not commonly used. In addition, everything is pronounced quite quickly and if you do not hear it, you can miss the whole essence of the dialog or monologue.
    Very Hard 刀語 Edo- era vocabulary, complicated samurai imagery with episodes that are unusually long for anime. The plot is not exactly episodical but each episode usually focuses on a different sword with a lot of historical and samurai terms. Interesting and witty dialogue that tends to go on and on making it difficult to follow.
    Very Hard 四畳半神話大系 Extremely fast-paced dialogues, constant references to literary works, obscure cultural references and philosophical considerations all dumped into one big pot. It's already difficult in English, so good luck in Japanese. Really good though.
    Very Hard 平家物語 Set in 12th century Japan and follows the Taira clan and Minamoto clan. All characters use old Japanese and archaic terms making it extremely hard to follow along without subtitles, and requires lots of experience and possibly some historical knowledge to be able to follow along with subtitles if you dont want to do a lot of lookups. This show isn't good immersion material for improving your language skills as its all archaic Japanese, but is a gold mine to mine kanji.
