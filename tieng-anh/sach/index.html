    This one is a little tricky because young adult books didn’t officially exist until the 1960s. That combined with the fact that for a book to be in the public domain it must remain in copyright for the life of the author plus seventy years (with some exceptions) means slim pickings. But books for children existed long before the official YA designation, and some books have retroactively been given the “middle-grade” or “young adult” designation by current parlance.

    For the youngest set: Picture Books

    —Almost all fairy tales and folk tales are public domain, but beware—these are not Disneyfied—

    • Andersen’s Fairy Tales - by Hans Christian Anderson
    • A Book of Myths - by Jean Lang
    • The Blue Fairy Book - by Andrew Lang
    • The Velveteen Rabbit - by Margery Williams

    Middle Grade (ages 8–12)

    —Age-appropriate but some language may be old-fashioned/didactic or contain words considered offensive today—

    • Peter Pan - by J.M. Barrie (fantasy)
    • The Wonderful Wizard of Oz - by Frank L. Baum (fantasy)
    • Little Women - by Louisa May Alcott (realistic fiction)
    • The Secret Garden - by Frances Hodgson Burnett (realistic fiction)
    • The Wind in the Willows - by Kenneth Grahame (animal adventure)
    • Alice’s Adventures in Wonderland - by Lewis Carroll (fantasy)
    • The Jungle Book - by Rudyard Kipling (animal adventure)
    • The Story of Dr. Doolittle - by Hugh Lofting (animal adventure)
    • Anne of Green Gables - * by Lucy Maude Montgomery (realistic fiction) *these start as MG but get YA as the series goes on
    • The Five Children and It - by E. Nesbit (fantasy)
    • Black Beauty - by Anna Sewell (animal adventure)

    • Heidi

    • by Johanna Spyri (realistic fiction)

    Young Adult (ages 12–16)

    —No warnings. These kids are old enough to figure it out—

    • The Legend of Sleepy Hollow by Washington Irving (spooky)
    • Treasure Island - by Robert Louis Stevenson (classic adventure, difficult language)
    • Uncle Tom’s Cabin - by Harriet Beecher Stowe (violent historical novel, but important message about slavery)
    • The Hound of Baskervilles - , Adventures of Sherlock Holmes by Sir Arthur Conan Doyle (mystery)
    • Twenty-Thousand Leagues Under the Sea - by Jules Verne (sci-fi)
    • War of the Worlds - by H.G. Wells (sci-fi)
    • Frankenstein - by Mary Shelley (spooky)
    • Dracula by Bram Stoker (spooky)
